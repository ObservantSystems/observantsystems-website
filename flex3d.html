ELI5: What are bands, scaffold tests, and the confidence checks?

Loaf of bread. FLEX slices bottom→top. Each slice measures “typical radial distance.”

Bands happen when many neighboring slices land on the same width “shelf.” That becomes a long bar in the band plot and a consistent color ring in 3D.

Scaffold test. An optional model asks whether measured radial distances can be summarized by repeated multiplicative steps. FLEX estimates the step from a disclosed search domain; no named constant is used as the default measurement reference.

Stability Sweep: jitter the knobs many times. If purity stays high and the scaffold step doesn’t drift, it’s “hard to shake.”

Null Test: keep big shape, scramble small wiggles. If nulls rarely beat real purity+fit, it’s less likely a mirage.

Slice Engine: intersects triangles with planes (not vertices), reducing mesh-density illusions.

Fixed Center Lens: holds one global center while measuring radius. A sphere should become extremely quiet when the fitted half-sphere cross-section is subtracted; vessels, heads, and skulls reveal how organized their departures from sphere-like behavior are.

Reciprocal traversal check. FLEX now compares mirrored regions around the belly/widest point. It asks whether expansion on one side is balanced by contraction on the other side, using log-ratio closure where log(r_left)+log(r_right)≈0. This is a structure-finding lens, not a proof of intent.

How to read “Radius vs Height”. The black line is the measured median radial distance at each height slice. The light gray lines are the spread (10th–90th percentile) — think handles / texture / local bumps. The green marks are the closest scaffold level (scaffold spine) under the active scaffold test.

A “band” is not “FLEX painted stripes.” It’s a run of heights where the measured geometry remains close to the same candidate state under the active lens, so the snapped level scaffold spine = scale·step^state stays stable. That can look cylindrical if the real shape is cylindrical, but it can also happen on tapered or curved regions: the black line can drift within the gray spread while still repeatedly returning to a nearby shelf.

Quick start: how to use this viewer

1. Load one or more STL files, then click Analyze All.

2. Start with the 3D View. Use it to see broad coherence, drift, texture, and transitions on the surface.

3. Scroll to State / band progression and Radius vs Height for the fastest structural read.

4. Use Cross-axis agreement when you want to compare how the object behaves across X, Y, and Z rather than assuming one privileged orientation.

5. Treat scaffold-based views as one lens among several. They can be revealing, but they are not the only thing FLEX is looking for.

What each main view is showing

3D View: surface coloring for state reuse, texture strength, or cross-axis convergence.

State / band progression: where slice states persist, switch, or cluster with height.

Radius vs Height: measured envelope versus the nearest active scaffold level.

Rule view (log-transformed): a transformed lens that makes candidate repeated spacing easier to inspect without treating it as settled in advance.

Fit error by height: where scaffold behavior matches cleanly and where it breaks down.

Scaffold state by height: whether a scaffold state stays stable, jumps, or reappears in separated regions.

Cross-axis agreement: where X, Y, and Z produce compatible signals, where they differ, and where transitions may align across orientations.

Scaffold deviation overlay: how measured radius departs from the active scaffold after landmark normalization.

Meridian sweep: angle-specific behavior, useful when lugs, handles, or asymmetry matter.
